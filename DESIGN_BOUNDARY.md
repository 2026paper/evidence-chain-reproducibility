# A/B/C design boundary

- Platform and deployment: A, B, and C were three separately published questionnaires on the **见数平台**.
- Assignment: the author confirms complete random allocation of participants among the three forms.
- Observed adherence: the final export contains 178 eligible response instances from 167 anonymous participant clusters; 10 clusters appear across forms. The primary GEE therefore clusters by participant, and a fixed single-form-participant sensitivity is reported.
- Item selection: the six materials were purposively selected disagreement cases, not a probability sample from all 810 items.
- Text relation: the public materials were rewritten in popular language while preserving the intended scientific meaning; they are not verbatim subsets of the upstream texts.

Accordingly, the defensible claim is a randomized reader-response comparison among A/B/C adaptations for these six cases. Participant randomization does not turn purposive case selection into corpus-wide random item sampling, and the adapted public texts are not an unchanged-stimulus API-versus-human validation set.
